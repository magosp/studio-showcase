The story

Some things don't decompress.

Wren Achterberg goes down alone to seal a collapsed hive-shaft, forty fathoms under a dead rig. The shaft is warm. Nothing that deep should be warm.

She comes up wearing it — a second skin of wet black chrome that keeps her breathing, keeps her still, and reads her vitals like a hand around a wrist. The dive company calls it a suit. It is not a suit.

It kept her alive on the way up. Now it wants to know what she is for.
